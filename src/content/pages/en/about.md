**M2** is a bilingual blog about digital sovereignty, open infrastructure, and the modern Linux desktop.

## What you'll find here

Articles are organized in three categories:

**Sovereignty** — How European regulation (ePrivacy, DMA, DSA, AI Act, eIDAS) affects personal autonomy online. Topics include client-side scanning, digital identity wallets, age verification, and the shifting balance between platform power and individual rights.

**Systems** — The architectural decisions that sustain open infrastructure: protocols, self-hosting, encryption, package management, DNS, VPNs, messaging, and version control. Each post focuses on how design choices affect long-term control and resilience.

**Environments** — Practical guides and real-world experience with the modern Linux desktop: Wayland vs X11, tiling window managers, rolling-release distributions, and the migration from macOS. No theory — just what works.

## Why bilingual?

Regulatory debates cross borders. Linux has a global community. Publishing in English and Spanish makes content accessible to readers in both languages without one dominating the other. Each post is written in its original language and both versions are kept in sync.
